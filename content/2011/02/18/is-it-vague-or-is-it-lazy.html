Reminds me of a passage Elisabeth Elliot wrote in “The Mark of a Man” about a “person” who swaggered up to her after one of her talks, hands thrust into jeans’ pockets, belligerent jaw thrust forward and working vigorously on a wad of gum.
\n“You know, I disagree with a lot of the stuff you say.”
\n“Okay. You’re allowed to disagree with me. But can you be specific? Tell me two things you disagree with.”
\nWell, I don’t know, I mean, like, you know, I just don’t like some of the stuff you say about women.”
\nIt wasn’t the time to point out the difference between not liking and not agreeing. I find that my students in the seminary often confuse the two. … But I met her on her terms.
\n“Okay. Fine. Tell me what you don’t like.”
\n“Well, like, you know, I just don’t like to think of myself as, like, you know, as a woman.” The gum was getting a terrific workout.
\n“Really? How do you like to think of yourself, then?”
\n“Just a person. You know?”
\nEverything about this person testified that she was trying. She was doing her best not to be a woman. But it was a poor show, because, come to think of it, there aren’t that many options.
